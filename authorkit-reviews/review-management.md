# Review Management

The Review Management interface gives you complete control over reader-submitted reviews. Access it through AuthorKit > Reviews in your WordPress dashboard to approve, reject, edit, or delete reviews before they appear on your site.

## Accessing Review Management

Navigate to **AuthorKit > Reviews** in your WordPress admin menu. You'll see a list of all reviews organized by status: Pending, Approved, and Rejected. The Pending tab displays reviews awaiting moderation, which is where you'll spend most of your time.

## Review Moderation Workflow

When a reader submits a review, it enters a **Pending** state and triggers an email notification to site administrators. This ensures no review goes live without your approval.

**Approving Reviews**: Hover over a pending review and click "Approve" to make it visible on your site. Approved reviews immediately appear on the corresponding book page, update the average rating, and increment the review count.

**Rejecting Reviews**: Click "Reject" to remove inappropriate, spam, or off-topic reviews. Rejected reviews remain in the system for your records but never display publicly. You can later approve a rejected review if you change your mind.

**Bulk Actions**: Select multiple reviews using checkboxes and apply bulk approve or reject actions to process many reviews quickly. This is especially useful after a book launch when you receive many reviews at once.

## Review Details

Each review in the management interface displays:

- **Reviewer Name**: The name provided during submission
- **Star Rating**: The 1-5 star rating given
- **Review Text**: The full written review content
- **Book Title**: Which book the review is for
- **Submission Date**: When the review was submitted
- **Status**: Current state (Pending, Approved, Rejected)
- **Verified Badge**: Whether the review is marked as a verified purchase

## Editing Reviews

Click on a review to open the detailed edit screen where you can:

- Modify the review text to correct typos or remove inappropriate language while preserving the feedback
- Adjust the star rating if needed
- Change the reviewer's display name
- Toggle the "Verified Purchase" badge
- Update the review status
- Add internal notes for your records

**Important**: While you can edit reviews, use this power responsibly. Readers trust that reviews reflect genuine opinions. Only make minor corrections for clarity or to remove inappropriate content.

## Verified Purchase Badges

Mark reviews as "Verified Purchases" to highlight feedback from readers who actually bought your book. This badge appears next to the reviewer's name and significantly increases trust with potential buyers.

To mark a review as verified, edit the review and check the "Verified Purchase" box. This is particularly useful when you receive reviews from direct customers through email or other channels and manually enter them into the system.

## Deleting Reviews

Permanently remove reviews by clicking "Delete" (or "Trash" depending on your WordPress settings). Use this sparingly, as deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Rejection is usually the better option since it preserves the review data.

## Review Notifications

Email notifications alert you immediately when new reviews are submitted. Configure notification settings in AuthorKit > Reviews > Settings to specify which email addresses receive alerts and customize the notification message format.

## Managing Review Count Limits

Free tier sites are limited to 3 reviews per book. When a book reaches this limit, the review submission form automatically hides for that specific book. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited reviews.

Check the review count for any book in the management interface. The system displays a warning when you're approaching the limit.
